# User Guide: Manoa CourseWise

## Welcome to Manoa CourseWise

Manoa CourseWise is a course review and scheduling platform designed for UH Mānoa students. Find honest reviews about ICS courses, understand course difficulty and workload, and make informed decisions about which courses to take.

---

## Getting Started

### Visiting the Site

1. Navigate to the landing page at the root URL (`/`)
2. You'll see the Manoa CourseWise hero section with:
   - **Search Bar**: Search for courses or professors
   - **Key Statistics**: Course count, review count, average rating, recommendation percentage
   - **Featured Courses**: Quick links to popular courses (ICS 311, MATH 242, ECON 300, BIOL 172)

---

## Finding Courses

### Using the Course Search

1. **Click "Search Courses"** in the navigation bar or navigate to `/courses/search`
2. You'll see all available ICS courses displayed in a grid layout

### Understanding Course Cards

Each course card displays:

- **Course Code** (e.g., "ICS 311") — highlighted in green
- **Course Name** (e.g., "Algorithms")  
- **Lead Professor** — the first instructor listed for this course
- **Overall Rating** — large green number (average of all review metrics)
- **Review Count** — total number of student reviews
- **Three Key Metrics** at the bottom:
  - **Difficulty** (1-5 scale) — How challenging is the material?
  - **Workload** (1-5 scale) — How much work outside of class?
  - **Clarity** (1-5 scale) — How clear are the lectures/materials?
- **Common Tags** — Badges showing frequent themes in student reviews

### Clicking a Course

Click any course card to view the **full detail page** with complete reviews, student feedback, and metrics.

## Viewing Course Details

### Course Detail Page

When you open a course, you'll see several sections:

#### **Header Section**
- **Course Code** (e.g., "ICS 311") — Large, in green
- **Course Name** (e.g., "Algorithms")
- **All Professors** — List of instructors who teach this course
- **Overall Rating** — Large green number (average rating)
- **Review Count** — Total reviews for this course

#### **Metrics Card**
Visual display of three core metrics:
- **Difficulty Average** (1-5) — How hard students found the material
- **Workload Average** (1-5) — Average amount of work required
- **Clarity Average** (1-5) — How well professors explained concepts (highlighted in green)

#### **Common Student Feedback**
- Shows the most frequent **tags** mentioned by students
- Examples: "Homework heavy", "Exam heavy", "Group projects", "Challenging", "Easy grading"
- Helps you understand what to expect from the course

#### **Student Reviews (Main Column)**
Each review includes:
- **Semester Taken** — When the student took the course (e.g., "Spring 2025")
- **Professor Name** — Which instructor taught them
- **Author** — Student name or "Anonymous"
- **Star Rating** — Large green number (1-5)
- **Written Review** — Detailed feedback about the course experience
- **Individual Metrics** — That student's personal scores (Difficulty, Workload, Clarity)
- **Tags** — Tags the student selected for their experience

#### **Write a Review Button**
- Click to submit your own course experience
- See the "Submitting Your Review" section below

#### **Course Schedule Card (Right Sidebar)**
Information about the course offering:
- **Typical Time** — When the course usually meets (currently "TBA" for most)
- **Best Semester** — Recommended semester to take it (currently "TBA")
- **Credits** — How many credits the course is worth (currently "TBA")
- **Add to My Schedule** — Button to save to your personal schedule

## Submitting Your Review

### How to Write a Review

1. **Navigate to any course detail page**
2. **Click "Write a Review"** button (below the reviews section)
3. **Fill out the review form** with the following information:

#### **Course Selection** (Required)
- **Course Code** — Dropdown list of available courses
- **Course Name** — Auto-populated based on your selection
- **Professor** — Select the instructor who taught you (dropdown filtered by course)

#### **Review Metrics** (Required - 1-5 Scale)
- **Difficulty** — How challenging was the material?
  - 1 = Very easy course
  - 3 = Moderate difficulty
  - 5 = Extremely difficult
- **Workload** — How much work outside of class?
  - 1 = Very light, minimal assignments
  - 3 = Moderate amount of work
  - 5 = Very heavy, lots of work
- **Clarity** — How clear were the lectures and materials?
  - 1 = Confusing, unclear instruction
  - 3 = Reasonably clear
  - 5 = Excellent, crystal clear

#### **Review Content** (Required)
- **Review Text** — Write your honest feedback about:
  - Teaching style and clarity
  - Assignment types and difficulty
  - Exam format and difficulty
  - Overall course experience
  - Advice for future students

#### **Optional Details**
- **Semester Taken** — When you took the course (e.g., "Spring 2025")
- **Tags** — Select up to 8 tags that describe your experience:
  - "Homework heavy"
  - "Exam heavy"
  - "Lecture heavy"
  - "Group projects"
  - "Challenging"
  - "Easy grading"
  - (Additional tags as available)
- **Anonymous** — Check this box to post your review anonymously

### Submitting Your Review

4. **Click "Submit Review"**
5. You'll be **redirected to the course detail page**
6. Your review will now appear in the **Student Reviews** section

## Understanding the Ratings

### The Three Core Metrics

#### **Difficulty (1-5)**
Measures how intellectually challenging the course material is.
- **1** = Very easy, minimal conceptual difficulty
- **2** = Easy, straightforward concepts
- **3** = Moderate, some challenging concepts
- **4** = Difficult, requires sustained effort
- **5** = Extremely difficult, very challenging material

#### **Workload (1-5)**
Measures the amount of work required outside of class (homework, projects, studying).
- **1** = Very light, 0-5 hours per week outside class
- **2** = Light, 5-10 hours per week
- **3** = Moderate, 10-15 hours per week
- **4** = Heavy, 15-20 hours per week
- **5** = Very heavy, 20+ hours per week

#### **Clarity (1-5)**
Measures how well professors explained concepts and organized materials.
- **1** = Very unclear, confusing instruction
- **2** = Unclear, some confusing moments
- **3** = Acceptable, mostly clear explanations
- **4** = Clear, good explanations
- **5** = Excellent, very clear and well-organized

### Overall Rating

The **Overall Rating** shown on course cards and detail pages is the average of all three metrics across all reviews. It's NOT a satisfaction rating, but rather a balanced view of course difficulty, workload, and teaching clarity.

## Tips for Using Manoa CourseWise

### ✅ Best Practices

1. **Read Multiple Reviews**
   - Don't rely on a single review; look for patterns across several reviews
   - One negative review doesn't mean the course is bad

2. **Consider Professor Differences**
   - Same course can be very different with different professors
   - Check if reviews mention specific teaching styles

3. **Pay Attention to Semester**
   - Course difficulty and workload can vary by semester
   - Spring vs. Fall offerings might differ significantly

4. **Look for Tag Patterns**
   - If many reviews say "Homework heavy", expect frequent assignments
   - Tags summarize common student experiences

5. **Consider Your Own Preferences**
   - A "heavy workload" course might be excellent if you prefer hands-on learning
   - A "challenging" course might be a great opportunity to grow

6. **Be Honest in Your Reviews**
   - Future students depend on accurate, thoughtful reviews
   - Specific details are more helpful than vague comments

### 💡 Reading Reviews Strategically

- **Start with the metrics**: Get a quick sense of difficulty/workload/clarity averages
- **Skim the common feedback tags**: See what students consistently mention
- **Read 3-5 recent reviews**: Get a balanced picture of recent student experiences
- **Look for actionable advice**: Reviews that mention specific challenges are most helpful

### 🎯 Choosing a Course Based on Reviews

**If you want an easier course:**
- Look for low Difficulty scores
- Check for "Easy grading" or similar positive tags
- Read reviews for encouraging feedback

**If you're looking for a challenging course:**
- Look for higher Difficulty scores
- Check tags like "Challenging" or "Rigorous"
- Read about what makes it challenging

**If you're concerned about workload:**
- Check the Workload metric specifically
- Look for reviews mentioning realistic time requirements
- Consider "Homework heavy" or "Exam heavy" tags

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How are courses added to the system?

The database is populated with ICS department courses and can be extended by the development team. Contact the Manoa CourseWise team to request new courses or departments.

### Can I edit my review after submitting?

Review editing functionality is not currently available. If you need to update your review, contact the development team.

### Is my review really anonymous?

Yes! When you check the "Anonymous" box, your email is not displayed with your review. You'll be listed as "Anonymous" instead of your name.

### How are courses ordered on the search page?

Courses are listed in order by course code (ICS 100, ICS 110P, ICS 111, etc.).

### What if I see incorrect information?

Contact the development team with details about what's incorrect. We appreciate help keeping our data accurate.

### Can I schedule courses through this app?

The "Add to My Schedule" button is planned for future development. For now, it's a placeholder for upcoming functionality.
